## Core Philosophy

Hormozi's approach is built on four pillars:

1. **Make Offers So Good They Sell Themselves** — The Grand Slam Offer
2. **Get Leads So Cheap It Feels Like Cheating** — Content-Market Fit
3. **Build Money Models That Print Cash** — Revenue Architecture
4. **Scale Without Breaking** — The 10-Stage Roadmap

## The Value Equation

The foundational formula for all Hormozi offers:

```
Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)
```

**How to apply:**
- Increase the dream outcome (what they really want)
- Increase perceived likelihood (proof, guarantees, testimonials)
- Decrease time delay (speed to result)
- Decrease effort & sacrifice (make it easy)

## The Grand Slam Offer

An offer with all four value drivers maximized:

1. **The Promise** — Specific, measurable outcome
2. **The Mechanism** — How you deliver it (unique, proprietary)
3. **The Price** — Relative to value, not cost
4. **The Guarantee** — Risk reversal
5. **The Scarcity** — Real urgency
6. **The Bonuses** — Stack value

### Offer Stacking Formula

```
Core Offer Value = $X
Bonus 1 Value = $Y
Bonus 2 Value = $Z
...
Total Value = $X + $Y + $Z + ...
Your Price = 10-25% of total stacked value
```

## The Money Model Framework

### Core Equation

```
Profit = (Price - Cost) × Volume - Overhead
```

### The Four Offer Types

All money models are built from four offer types:

1. **Attraction Offers** — Turn strangers into customers (free, discount, premium)
2. **Upsell Offers** — Get people to spend more cash
3. **Downsell Offers** — Get people to say yes when they would have said no
4. **Continuity Offers** — Keep people buying month after month

### The Three Money Model Types

1. **Attraction Model** — Low price, high volume, break-even or loss on front end
2. **Continuity Model** — Recurring revenue, focus on retention
3. **Ascension Model** — High-ticket backend, maximize LTV

### The Three Levers of Customer Financed Acquisition (CFA)

1. **Cost To Acquire a Customer (CAC)** — What you spend to get one customer
2. **Lifetime Gross Profit (LTGP)** — Total profit from one customer over their lifetime
3. **Payback Period (PPD)** — How long until you recover CAC

**The CFA Formula:**
```
LTGP = Lifetime Gross Profit
CAC = Cost To Acquire a Customer
PPD = Payback Period (days/months)
```

**Levels of Customer Financed Acquisition:**
- **Level 1:** CAC recovered in first transaction (immediate)
- **Level 2:** CAC recovered within 30 days
- **Level 3:** CAC recovered within 90 days
- **Level 4:** CAC recovered within 12 months
- **Level 5:** CAC never recovered (bad business)

### The Value Grid

Map your offers on two axes:
- **X-axis:** Price (low → high)
- **Y-axis:** Frequency (one-time → recurring)

| | One-Time | Recurring |
|---|---|---|
| **Low Price** | Tripwire | Entry continuity |
| **High Price** | High-ticket | Premium continuity |

Fill every quadrant for maximum revenue.

### Four Steps To Picking The Right Offer

When adding any offer to your money model:

1. **Right Stage** — Does it fit the money model stage? (Attraction = new customers, Upsell = increase AOV, Continuity = retention)
2. **Right Problem** — Does it solve a problem you can solve with existing resources?
3. **Right Way** — Does it solve the problem how the customer wants it solved?
4. **Right Time** — Is the customer hungry for this solution right now?

### Advanced Offer Stacking

**Attraction Offer Variations:**
- Free Presentations (webinars, workshops)
- Freemium (free tier, paid upgrades)
- Free Pick Your Price (pay what you want)

**Upsell Offer Variations:**
- Free With Alternate Revenue Stream (free product, monetize differently)
- Immediate Upsell (offered right after purchase)
- Delayed Upsell (offered after results)

**Continuity Offer Variations:**
- Lifetime Upgrades (pay once, get all future updates)
- Lifetime Discounts (pay once, get permanent discount)
- Discount + One-Time Fee (reduced monthly + setup fee)

## Hook Writing Framework

### The Hook Formula

```
Hook = Pattern Interrupt + Promise of Value
```

### Types of Hooks

1. **Verbal Hooks** — Words, headlines, opening lines
2. **Nonverbal Hooks** — Images, thumbnails, body language

### How Hooks Work

A hook has two parts:
1. **The Call Out** — Grabs attention (pattern interrupt)
2. **The Promise** — Delivers value (what they'll get)

The call out grabs attention in order to deliver the promise.

### Hook Templates

**Result Hooks:**
- "How I [achieved result] in [timeframe] without [common sacrifice]"
- "I went from [before] to [after] in [timeframe] using [mechanism]"
- "The exact [method] I used to [result]"

**Curiosity Hooks:**
- "What [authority] doesn't want you to know about [topic]"
- "The [adjective] truth about [topic]"
- "Why most [target audience] will never [desired outcome]"

**Contrast Hooks:**
- "I was [negative state] until I discovered [mechanism]"
- "The difference between [common approach] and [better approach]"
- "Stop doing [common mistake]. Start doing [better action]."

**Story Hooks:**
- "In [year], I was [situation]. Then [event] changed everything."
- "The [number] [things] that [result]"
- "When [person] told me [statement], I didn't believe them. Then..."

**Direct Hooks:**
- "Want [desired outcome]? Read this."
- "If you're [target audience], this is for you."
- "Stop scrolling if you want [benefit]."

### The Shout Method

When you're weak, appear strong. When you're strong, appear weak.

- If you're rich, look like an everyday guy
- If you're new, show hustle and hunger
- If you're established, show humility

### Weekly Hook Generation System

**Day 1:** Write 50 hooks
**Day 2:** Select top 10
**Day 3:** Create ad variations
**Day 4:** Launch test campaign
**Day 5:** Analyze results
**Day 6:** Double winners, kill losers
**Day 7:** Document learnings

## Retention & Churn Framework

### The 5 Horsemen of Retention

1. **Results** — Do customers get what they paid for?
   - Track attendance, usage, outcomes
   - If they don't get results, they leave
   - Example: Gym members who came 3x/week stayed; those who came <2x/week churned

2. **Engagement** — Are they actively using/participating?
   - Measure logins, visits, interactions
   - Low engagement = early churn signal
   - Proactive outreach when engagement drops

3. **Community** — Do they feel belonging?
   - Member events, group activities, social connections
   - People stay where they feel they belong
   - Build rituals and shared experiences

4. **Progress** — Can they see improvement?
   - Track and display metrics
   - Celebrate milestones
   - Visual progress beats invisible progress

5. **Surprise** — Do you exceed expectations?
   - Unexpected bonuses, personal touches
   - "Wow" moments create loyalty
   - Under-promise, over-deliver

### Churn Rate Formula

```
Churn Rate = Customers Lost / Starting Customer Pool
```

Example: 100 customers - 95 remaining = 5 churned
Churn = 5/100 = 5%

**At 15% monthly churn, you lose 83% of customers in a year.**

### The Churn Checklist

Instead of asking "How do I retain customers?" ask "What would make ALL my customers leave?" Then do the opposite.

Questions to ask:
- What if we stopped delivering results?
- What if we stopped engaging with customers?
- What if we destroyed the community?
- What if customers couldn't see progress?
- What if we never surprised them?

### Retention Math

```
If average customer stays 12 months at $100/mo:
LTV = 12 × $100 = $1,200

If you reduce churn from 10% to 5%:
Average stay increases from 10 months to 20 months
LTV doubles from $1,000 to $2,000
```

### The Perfect Continuity Offer

**Buy 10 Get 2 Free:**
- If average stay > 12 months, offer "buy 10 months, get 2 free"
- Customer pays for 10, gets 12 months
- You get cash upfront, they get a deal
- Reduces churn because they're committed

**Annual Prepay Discount:**
- Monthly: $200/mo
- Annual: $1,500/yr (equivalent to $125/mo)
- Customer saves $900
- You get cash now, lock in commitment

## Branding Framework

### The Bouquet Metaphor

Your brand is a bouquet of associations:
- What you say (advertising)
- What you do (actions)
- What others say (reputation)

Just as tying many flowers together forms a bouquet, tying products, values, experiences, and people together forms a brand.

**How to narrow your brand:** Talk about one specific thing and nothing else. Like having a bouquet of only red roses.

**How to expand your brand:** Bring in all sorts of flowers. Talk about broader categories.

### The 3 Brand Components

1. **What We Say** — This is advertising, self-promotion, direct communication
2. **What Other People Say** — What others say matters way more than what you say
3. **What They Experience Themselves** — A person's own opinion of their experience has the most weight

### Audience Selection Criteria

1. Growing market (not shrinking)
2. Has money (can afford your offer)
3. Easy to target (reachable)
4. In pain (desperate for solution)

### How to Start, Grow, then Pivot Your Brand

**Start:** Pick one narrow audience and one specific problem
**Grow:** Expand to adjacent audiences and problems
**Pivot:** When the market shifts, reposition your bouquet

**The Rule:** When you sell a product, polarization occurs whether you want it to or not. Embrace it.

## Lead Nurture Framework

### The Follow-Up Grid

| Channel | Timing | Purpose |
|---------|--------|---------|
| Text | Immediate | Response, scheduling |
| Email | Same day | Details, value |
| Phone | 24-48 hrs | Personal touch |
| DM | Ongoing | Relationship |

### Speed To Lead

**The 3 Speeds:**

1. **Speed To First Contact** — How fast you respond after they express interest
   - Gold standard: Contact ALL leads in 5 minutes or less
   - The slower you contact, the more times you have to contact before they buy
   - The faster you contact, the fewer times you have to contact
   - Litmus test: If you're not getting "man that was fast!" at least once a day, you're too slow

2. **Speed To First Appointment** — How fast you get them scheduled
   - Reduce time slots, create urgency
   - Same-day or next-day appointments convert highest
   - The longer the gap, the higher the no-show rate

3. **Speed Of Response** — How fast you reply to their messages
   - Reply to texts/emails within minutes, not hours
   - Fast response = high interest = easy sale
   - Slow response = low interest = hard sale

### Response Rate Optimization

- Speed to lead (respond in <5 minutes)
- Multiple touchpoints (7+ contacts)
- Value-first follow-up (not just "checking in")
- Clear next steps every time

### The Lead Nurture Sequence

**Immediate (0-5 min):**
- Text: "Hey [Name], saw you [action]. When's a good time to chat?"

**Same Day:**
- Email: Value + context + clear CTA
- Phone call if no text response

**Day 2-3:**
- Text: Follow up on value, offer specific time slots
- Email: Case study or testimonial

**Day 4-7:**
- Phone: Personal check-in
- Email: FAQ or objection handler

**Week 2+:**
- Weekly touch: New value, new angle
- Monthly: Long-term nurture content

**The Rule:** Every contact must provide value or move them to the next step. Never "just checking in."

## Pricing Framework

### My Rules For Raising Prices

1. **Pair with a launch** — Raise prices within 90 days of a product launch
2. **Add value first** — Show what you've added before raising prices
3. **Communicate clearly** — Explain why, not just what
4. **Grandfather existing** — Lock current customers at old price for a period
5. **Test on new customers first** — See if new price works before rolling out

### Price Raise Justification

When raising prices, communicate:
1. What you currently do (value delivered)
2. What you've added (new improvements)
3. Why the increase (sustainability/growth)
4. The new price (with timeline)
5. Grandfather option (for existing customers)

### The Perfect Price Raise Math

```
If you double your price and close 20% fewer deals:
- Old: 10 customers × $5,000 = $50,000
- New: 8 customers × $10,000 = $80,000
- Result: 60% more revenue with 20% less work
```

### Price Raise Email Template

```
Subject: [Important] Changes to [Product] Pricing

Hi [Name],

We've invested in:
• [Improvement 1]
• [Improvement 2]
• [Improvement 3]

Effective [Date], pricing will be [New Price].
As an existing customer, you're locked at [Current Price] for [Period].

Questions? Just reply.

[Signature]
```

### Price Raise Letter Structure (Physical Mail)

**Section 1: R — Remind them of value already provided**
- Feature A helped you do Y
- Feature B is great and you use it daily

**Section 2: WHAT WE CURRENTLY DO**
- List current value delivered
- Specific features, services, improvements

**Section 3: WHAT IS COMING**
- New improvements being made
- Why these improvements matter
- How they benefit the customer

**Section 4: THE NEW PRICE**
- Clear statement of new price
- When it takes effect
- Grandfather terms for existing customers

**Section 5: PS**
- If B2C: "If this will materially affect your ability to buy groceries, let me know and we'll work something out"
- If B2B: "If this materially impacts your business, let me know and we'll work something out"

## Lifetime Value Framework

### The Crazy Eight

Eight ways to increase customer lifetime value:

1. **Increase Prices** — Raise prices on new customers
2. **Decrease Costs** — Reduce cost of delivery
3. **Increase # of Purchases** — Get them to buy more often
4. **Cross-Sell Something Different** — Sell complementary products
5. **Sell More (Increase Quantity)** — Sell larger quantities
6. **Sell Better (Increase Quality)** — Upgrade to premium versions
7. **Downsell Fewer (Lower Quantity)** — Offer smaller packages
8. **Downsell Lower Quality** — Offer basic versions

### LTV Calculation

```
LTV = Average Order Value × Purchase Frequency × Customer Lifespan

Or:
LTGP = Lifetime Gross Profit = Total Revenue - Total Cost of Delivery
```

**Example:**
- Average order: $100
- Purchase frequency: 4x/year
- Lifespan: 3 years
- LTV = $100 × 4 × 3 = $1,200

### LTV Maximization Strategy

**Front End:**
- Get customers at break-even or small profit
- Focus on volume and speed

**Back End:**
- Upsell immediately after purchase
- Cross-sell complementary products
- Offer continuity/recurring options
- Save high-ticket for when trust is built

**The Math:**
```
Business #1: 190 sales × $50 profit = $9,500
Business #2: 5% of 190 = 9.5 sales × $9,500 profit = $90,250

Same customers, 9.5x more profitable
```
